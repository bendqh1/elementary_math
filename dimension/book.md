In the real world there is width, height, and depth.<br>
In mathematics and computer graphics there is a coordinate system of width, height, and depth (x, y, z).

These three axes are independent in the sense that each axis is an independent direction in space;<br>
It is possible to move along it without depending on the others (for example, right without up or forward). This is not a dependency of definition or word order, but a form of geometric freedom of movement.

In the real world, thickness is the simplest example of depth.<br>
In contrast, in mathematics and computer graphics, thickness is a measurement along the depth axis.

* In a two-dimensional computer game, **all** objects have only width and height.
* In a three-dimensional computer game, **all** objects have width, height, and depth.
* In a 2.5D computer game, there is width and height, but also at least one independent object with depth, which is the exception rather than the general rule.
